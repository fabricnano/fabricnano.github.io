FabricNano is supercharging cellular reactions. We are a chemical startup at the intersection of nano and biotechnology in London, UK. We generate designs for the optimal co-localization of enzymes on a fabric made of DNA. With our intelligent designs, we prototype these systems for clients to verify claims of near 100x chemical reaction speed ups. We have a number of large industrial clients and are working on the first ever prototypes of this technology for commercial use. The company has already filed a number of patents and expects to make scientific discoveries, while further optimizing design parameters for a range of client specific chemical reactions.

What does a DNA nanotechnologist do at FabricNano?

The nanotechnologists bring FabricNano designs to reality in the lab. Our technologists are master chefs writing a cookbook of recipes, and the wet lab is their experimental kitchen. At FabricNano, we aim to push each other by hitting well-defined objectives (long hours not necessary), but within this objectives first framework the nanotechnologist have most of the fun experimenting with structural DNA concepts in the lab; the results of these experiments are passed back to the companies central AI algorithms to inform the next experiment. Our nanotechnologists run tests that show just how powerful supercharging cellular reactions can be in practice. In weekly company meetings, we rely heavily on the practical input and ideas of our nanotechnologists in the lab.

Why we hire DNA nanotechnologists?

As a DNA nanotechnologists, you will become a world leading expert at wet lab protein-DNA conjugation. Additionally, you will be asked to voice your strong opinion on design parameters when working with the cofounder CTO on simulation model updates.

It’s incredible how fast things move when you are on the right track! We are currently securing an additional 2Million GBP in fundraising from the best venture capitalists in the world.

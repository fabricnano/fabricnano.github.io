A World-Changing Company

FabricNano is supercharging cellular reactions. We are a venture backed chemical startup using nanotechnology and biotechnology to synthesize chemicals. Our algorithms design for efficient enzyme co-location on a fabric made of DNA. Using our intelligent designs, we prototype these systems for a target client molecule to verify our claims of near 100x chemical reaction speed. At FabricNano, we are building the future of industrial chemical production.

What We Do

FabricNano is reducing production bottlenecks and enabling commercial viability across a wide range of target molecules. Our examples include fermented PLA bioplastics to replace oil-based plastics dumped in the oceans, fermented amino acids to feed a growing agricultural demand, enzymatic synthesis of the grapefruit fragrance to save 100k tons of those delicious fruits for humans, plus countless other consumer-driven markets constrained by high production costs. Our team is always looking for individuals with robust experience and a deep interest in the science of nanotechnology, enzymology, micro and molecular biology. We are constantly looking for new chemical markets and individuals with industry domain experience in the product molecules/clients we are targeting next. We are challenging the traditional use of living cells in chemical synthesis and we are learning new science along the way.

What You Will Experience/Core Responsibilities

At FabricNano, we aim to push each other by hitting well-defined objectives. Willingness to accept and respond to feedback, as well as providing it are critical. In weekly company meetings, we rely heavily on the practical input and ideas of our scientists in the lab.

  • Biologists at FabricNano will become world-leading experts at unique protein binding residues and chemistries.
  • You will be responsible for leading entire projects and owning their outcomes from start to finish.
  • You will be asked to voice your strong opinion on critical features of the protein-DNA system you are studying. You will inform the cofounder/CTO, so that they can be implemented in our simulation protocols.
  • You will need to be innovative, self-motivated, and collaborative in a research environment.
  • Our biologists help characterize residue binding and chemistries that are mission critical to the multi-enzyme system.
